My Story

My practice sits at the intersection of teaching, technology and inclusion. I've worked with disengaged youth, youth justice students, and young people with complex needs—experiences that shape how I design AI tools for real classrooms.

As a teacher, I saw how admin burden, fragmented data and generic platforms pulled attention away from students. As a developer, I built tools to fix that—first small scripts, then tools like TrackMe-Ed, AdjustMe and ClassWork-Ed for use across sites.

Every project starts with: How does this make a teacher's life easier while strengthening inclusion? If the answer isn't clear, we redesign before writing code.

Humble Learning & AI Compliance

I don't just bring success stories—I bring lived experience of managing risk when things don't go to plan.

Not every project has gone perfectly. I've seen pilots land badly when staff were overloaded or the "shiny AI idea" didn't match classroom reality. Those experiences matter.

I bring lived experience of managing risk: slowing or pausing roll-outs, re-designing workflows with teachers, and tightening data access, consent and storage.

When we work together, we talk honestly about AI compliance, safety and guardrails from the start—what data is in play, who can see it, and what we'll do if something isn't working. The goal is a process that keeps students and staff safe while we learn and adjust.
